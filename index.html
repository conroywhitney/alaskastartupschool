Alaska Startup School

Validate Your Business Idea in 8 Weeks

Alaska Startup School is an 8-week program is designed to save you time, money, and heartache by guiding you through the necessary steps to validate your business idea. By the end, you will either have a clear roadmap to profitibility; or you will have learned that your idea does not have a viable market. Either way, it's a win-win scenario.

Build Something People Want

Instead of building something and then trying to sell it, we will first see if there is a real problem that actual customers would be willing to pay you to solve for them. We will then determine the Minimal Viable Product (MVP) that we need to build to start getting paying customers in weeks instead of months.

Exploring Into the Unknown

This program is intended for people who want to pursue ideas which have a lot of uncertainty about what exactly the product should be and whether there is a real market for it (e.g., a new restaurant reservation application, or accounting software). It is less helpful businesses with proven business models and clear markets (e.g., opening a new restaurant, or starting an accounting firm.)

Don't Go It Alone

You can either come in with an existing team (up to 4 people), or be a "solopreneur" (just yourself). Either way, the Alaska Startup School instructors, mentors, and peer community will be there as an extension of your team to help you navigate your unique idea circumstances at every step of the way.

Battle-Tested Cirriculum

The cirriculum is based on the Lean Launchpad which was professionally developed by world-renown startup experts. Following Lean Startup methodologies, we will use Customer Development techniques to iterate on your Business Model Canvas to achieve Product/Market Fit.

Your Part-Time Summer Job

You will spend ~20 hours/week between May 6th, 2018 and June 30th, 2018. During the 8-week process, we will:

  • Watch a free online video course called How to Build a Startup by the creator of the Lean Launchpad cirriculum
  • Iterate on your Business Model Canvas, which is the ever-evolving artifact that represents the core of your business
  • Have twice-weekly group video calls where each business presents their progress and leanings
  • Read The Mom Test and The Lean Startup, two books which will help change your perspective on how to go about starting a business
  • Have ongoing conversations in a group Slack channel where we can ask questions, discuss ideas, and get feedback
  • Talk with ~50 customers to learn what it's like to be them, to understand their problem, to learn what solutions they have tried, and to determine what features to build
  • Create a prototype of the final product, as well as a "smokescreen" website where customers can sign up ahead of time to purchase your product once you launch
  • Determine an actionable plan for bringing your product into the real world after the 8-week class is over.

Online, Alaska-Wide

This program is for Alaska residents all over the state. For that reason, it is held completely online. The only requirements are that you have a fast enough internet connection that you are able to participate in twice-weekly video calls.

Cirriculum

Week 1: Intro & Goals

May 6th - 12th, 2018

  • Exploring the concepts of Lean Startup, Customer Development, & the Business Model Canvas
  • Identifying fundamental hypotheses which need to be tested
  • Creating an initial Customer Interview script
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 2: Value Proposition

May 13th - 19th, 2018

  • Exploring your Value Propositions in terms of pains you are solving & gains you are creating for your customers
  • Identifying the Mininimum Viable Product (MVP) required to prove or disprove the validity of your Value Proposition hypotheses
  • Creating an initial Mockup to use during Customer Interviews
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 3: Customer Segments

May 20th - 26th, 2018

  • Exploring the different types of Customer Segments (users, influencers, recommenders, decision makers, economic buyers, & saboteurs)
  • Identifying your target Customer Segements and their relative Value Propositions
  • Improving your Mockup based on customer feedback
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 4: Channels

May 27th - June 2nd, 2018

  • Exploring the different types of distribution Channels (direct, indirect, OEM, virtual, & physical)
  • Identifying your target Channels including user flow and cost
  • Creating a Smokescreen website with feature sets & screenshots
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 5: Customer Relationships

June 3rd - 9th, 2018

  • Exploring the concepts of customer Lifetime Value (LTV), Customer Acquisition Costs (CAC), and "Get, Keep, Grow"
  • Identifying your Marketing, Sales, and Onboarding funnels
  • Creating multiple online Marketing Campaign to test costs & relative conversion rates
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 6: Revenue Model

June 10th - 16th, 2018

  • Exploring the different types of Revenue Models (direct sales, licensing, subscription, etc.) & Pricing Strategies
  • Identifying and testing your Revenue Model & Pricing Strategy
  • Acquire your first "Paying" Customers through your Smokescreen website
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 7: Activities, Resources, & Costs

June 17th - 23rd, 2018

  • Exploring concepts of Resources (financial, physical, intellectual, & human capital); Activities (manufacturing, supply chain, & problem solving); and Costs (fixed vs. variable)
  • Identifying your core Activities, Resources, & Costs
  • Creating a set of User Stories for your MVP feature set
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers

Week 8: Partners, Wrap-Up, & Next Steps

June 24th - 30th, 2018

  • Exploring different types of Partners (strategic alliances, competition, joint ventures, buyers, suppliers, & licensees)
  • Identifying which Partners would be a good fit for your business
  • Creating a set of steps with a timeline to Build your product
  • Interviewing 10-15 customers
